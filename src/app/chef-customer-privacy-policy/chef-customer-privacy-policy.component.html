2. The data Chefs collect about you

Personal data, or personal information, means any information about an individual from which that person can be identified. It does not include data where the identity has been removed (anonymous data).

Chefs may collect, use, store and transfer different kinds of personal data about you which they have grouped together as follows:

· Identity Data includes first name, last name, username or similar identifier, marital status, title, date of birth and gender.

· Contact Data includes venue address for the Personal Chef Services, email address and telephone numbers.

· Transaction Data includes details of the Personal Chef Services you have purchased via Cook-a-Doddle.

· Profile Data includes your username, Orders made by you for the Personal Chef Services, your interests, preferences and feedback.

· Marketing and Communications Data includes your feedback and preferences in receiving marketing from third parties and your communication preferences.

· Special Categories of Personal Data includes information about your health, race or ethnicity or religious beliefs.

Chefs do not normally collect any other Special Categories of Personal Data about you (this includes details about your race or ethnicity, philosophical beliefs, sex life, sexual orientation, political opinions, trade union membership, and genetic and biometric data). Nor do Chefs collect any information about criminal convictions and offences.

If you fail to provide personal data

Where Chefs need to collect personal data by law, or under the terms of a contract Chefs have with you, and you fail to provide that data when requested, Chefs may not be able to perform the contract Chefs have or are trying to enter into with you (for example, to provide you with Personal Chef Services). In this case, Chefs may have to cancel a service you have ordered but Chefs will notify you if this is the case at the time.

Chefs will get your express opt-in consent before they share your personal data with any third party for marketing purposes.

Please note that Chefs may process your personal data without your knowledge or consent, in compliance with the above rules, where this is required or permitted by law.

Chefs have put in place appropriate security measures to prevent your personal data from being accidentally lost, used or accessed in an unauthorised way, altered or disclosed. 8. Data retention

How long will you use my personal data for?

Chefs will only retain your personal data for as long as reasonably necessary to fulfil the purposes Chefs collected it for, including for the purposes of satisfying any legal, regulatory, tax, accounting or reporting requirements. Chefs may retain your personal data for a longer period in the event of a complaint or if Chefs reasonably believe there is a prospect of litigation in respect to their relationship with you.

To determine the appropriate retention period for personal data, Chefs consider the amount, nature and sensitivity of the personal data, the potential risk of harm from unauthorised use or disclosure of your personal data, the purposes for which Chefs process your personal data and whether Chefs can achieve those purposes through other means, and the applicable legal, regulatory, tax, accounting or other requirements.

In some circumstances you can ask Chefs to delete your data: see [your legal rights] below for further information.

In some circumstances Chefs will anonymise your personal data (so that it can no longer be associated with you) for research or statistical purposes, in which case Chefs may use this information indefinitely without further notice to you.

10. Glossary

LAWFUL BASIS

Legitimate Interest means the Chefs’ business interest in conducting and managing their business to enable them to give you the best service and the best and most secure experience. Chefs make sure they consider and balance any potential impact on you (both positive and negative) and your rights before they process your personal data for their legitimate interests. Chefs do not use your personal data for activities where their interests are overridden by the impact on you (unless Chefs have your consent or are otherwise required or permitted to by law). You can obtain further information about how Chefs assess their legitimate interests against any potential impact on you in respect of specific activities by contacting the Chef or Cook-a-Doddle.

Performance of Contract means processing your data where it is necessary for the performance of a contract to which you are a party or to take steps at your request before entering into such a contract.

Comply with a legal obligation means processing your personal data where it is necessary for compliance with a legal obligation that Chefs are subject to.

YOUR LEGAL RIGHTS

You have the right to:

Request access to your personal data (commonly known as a "data subject access request"). This enables you to receive a copy of the personal data Chefs hold about you and to check that Chefs are lawfully processing it.

Request correction of the personal data that Chefs hold about you. This enables you to have any incomplete or inaccurate data Chefs hold about you corrected, though Chefs may need to verify the accuracy of the new data you provide to them.

Request erasure of your personal data. This enables you to ask Chefs to delete or remove personal data where there is no good reason for them continuing to process it. You also have the right to ask Chefs to delete or remove your personal data where you have successfully exercised your right to object to processing (see below), where Chefs may have processed your information unlawfully or where Chefs are required to erase your personal data to comply with local law. Note, however, that Chefs may not always be able to comply with your request of erasure for specific legal reasons which will be notified to you, if applicable, at the time of your request.

Object to processing of your personal data where Chefs are relying on a legitimate interest (or those of a third party) and there is something about your particular situation which makes you want to object to processing on this ground as you feel it impacts on your fundamental rights and freedoms. You also have the right to object where Chefs are processing your personal data for direct marketing purposes. In some cases, Chefs may demonstrate that Chefs have compelling legitimate grounds to process your information which override your rights and freedoms.

Request restriction of processing of your personal data. This enables you to ask Chefs to suspend the processing of your personal data in the following scenarios:

· If you want Chefs to establish the data's accuracy.

· Where Chefs’ use of the data is unlawful but you do not want them to erase it.

· Where you need Chefs to hold the data even if Chefs no longer require it as you need it to establish, exercise or defend legal claims.

· You have objected to Chefs’ use of your data but Chefs need to verify whether they have overriding legitimate grounds to use it.

Request the transfer of your personal data to you or to a third party. Chefs will provide to you, or a third party you have chosen, your personal data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format. Note that this right only applies to automated information which you initially provided consent for Chefs to use or where Chefs used the information to perform a contract with you.

Withdraw consent at any time where Chefs are relying on consent to process your personal data. However, this will not affect the lawfulness of any processing carried out before you withdraw your consent. If you withdraw your consent, Chefs may not be able to provide certain services to you. Chefs will advise you if this is the case at the time you withdraw your consent.
